# Salivary gland stones

## Question
What causes salivary gland stones?

### Answer
It's not clear what causes salivary gland stones, so there's no way to prevent them.

They're not usually linked with any other health problems.

Salivary stones do not usually come back. But if you keep getting them you might need to have 1 of your salivary glands removed.

## Question
What should I know about salivary gland stones and check if youve got salivary gland stones?

### Answer
Most stones appear below your tongue. They are usually in one of the glands that supply saliva to your mouth. You cannot always see them.

## Question
What should I know about salivary gland stones and things you can try yourself?

### Answer
You can try to remove the stone by doing things to increase saliva production, such as:

- sucking on a lemon or lemon drops
- drinking plenty of water
- massaging around the stone

If you have pain and swelling:

- take paracetamol or ibuprofen
- suck ice cubes or ice lollies

The symptoms will not go away until the stone has been removed.

## Question
What should I know about salivary gland stones and treatment to remove the stones?

### Answer
Small salivary gland stones sometimes fall out on their own. You’ll continue to have symptoms until the stone falls out or is removed.

A GP may attempt to remove the stone with a thin, blunt instrument.

If that's not possible, you may need to have the stone removed in hospital.
