# Shoulder pain

## Question
What should I know about shoulder pain and things that can make shoulder pain worse?

### Answer
You can make your shoulder pain worse if you:

- completely stop using your shoulder - this can stop it getting better
- do things that seem to make it worse
- make up your own strenuous exercises or use heavy gym equipment
- slouch when sitting - do not roll your shoulders or bring your neck forward

## Question
What should I know about shoulder pain and treatment from a gp?

### Answer
A GP will examine you to work out what's causing your shoulder pain. They might send you for tests to check the cause, such as an x-ray.

They'll suggest a treatment based on the cause, for example:

- stronger medicine or injections to ease pain and swelling
- physiotherapy or exercises to do at home
- things to avoid to stop the pain getting worse or returning
- seeing a specialist for tests or treatment

Physiotherapy for shoulder pain
The number of physiotherapy sessions depends on the cause of your shoulder pain.
Follow the advice and exercises your physiotherapist gives you. This can help you avoid having surgery.
If you're still in pain after your sessions end, go back to your GP. They might suggest another treatment or refer you to a specialist.

## Question
What should I know about shoulder pain and when to get medical help for shoulder pain?

### Answer
A pharmacist can usually help with shoulder pain.

Ask them to suggest:

- the best painkiller - this might be tablets, or a cream or gel you rub on the skin
- other ideas for pain relief and things you can buy to help, like heat and cold packs

They might advise contacting your GP if you need to.
