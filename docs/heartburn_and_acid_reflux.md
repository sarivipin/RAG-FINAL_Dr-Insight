# Heartburn and acid reflux

## Question
What are the symptoms of heartburn and acid reflux?

### Answer
The main symptoms of acid reflux are:

- heartburn - a burning feeling in your chest
- an unpleasant sour taste in your mouth, caused by stomach acid

You may also have:

- a cough or hiccups that keep coming back
- a hoarse voice
- bad breath
- bloating and nausea (feeling sick)

Symptoms are often worse after you eat, lie down or bend over.

## Question
What causes heartburn and acid reflux?

### Answer
Lots of people get heartburn from time to time. There's often no obvious reason why.

Sometimes it's caused or made worse by:

- some food and drink - such as coffee, tomatoes, alcohol, chocolate and fatty or spicy foods
- carrying extra weight
- smoking
- pregnancy
- stress and anxiety
- some medicines, such as anti-inflammatory painkillers (such as ibuprofen)
- a hiatus hernia - when part of your stomach moves up into your chest

## Question
What should I know about heartburn and acid reflux and treating heartburn and acid reflux?

### Answer
Your GP may prescribe a medicine called a proton pump inhibitor (PPI). This reduces how much acid your stomach makes. PPIs include omeprazole and lansoprazole .

You'll usually need to take this type of medicine for 4 or 8 weeks, depending on how serious your acid reflux is.

Go back to the GP if your symptoms come back after you stop taking your medicine. You may need a long-term prescription.

Tests and surgery for heartburn and acid reflux
If medicines do not help or your symptoms are severe, your GP may refer you to a specialist for:
- tests to find out what's causing your symptoms, such as a gastroscopy (a thin tube with a camera to look inside your throat and stomach)
- an operation on your stomach to stop acid reflux - called a laparoscopic fundoplication

## Question
What should I know about heartburn and acid reflux and when to see a pharmacist?

### Answer
Speak to a pharmacist for advice if you keep getting heartburn.

They can recommend medicines called antacids .

These can help ease your symptoms. It's best to take them with food or soon after eating. This is when you're most likely to get heartburn. They may also work for longer if you take them with food.
