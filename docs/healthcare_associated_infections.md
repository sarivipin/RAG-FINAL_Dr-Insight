## Question
What should I know about healthcare associated infections and c auris?

### Answer

## Question
What should I know about healthcare associated infections and c diff?

### Answer
Clostridioides difficile (C. diff) are bacteria (bugs) that live in the gut. C. diff spreads more easily inside a hospital.

**Section:** c_diff

**Source:** https://www2.hse.ie/conditions/healthcare-associated-infections/#c-diff

## Question
What should I know about healthcare associated infections and iv line infection?

### Answer
An intravenous (IV) line is used to give you blood, fluid or medicine. An IV line can make it easier for harmful bugs to spread.

**Section:** iv_line_infection

**Source:** https://www2.hse.ie/conditions/healthcare-associated-infections/#iv-line-infection

## Question
What should I know about healthcare associated infections and norovirus?

### Answer
Norovirus is the most common tummy bug in Ireland. It's known as the 'vomiting bug' and spreads easily from person-to-person.

**Section:** norovirus

**Source:** https://www2.hse.ie/conditions/healthcare-associated-infections/#norovirus
