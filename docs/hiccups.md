# Hiccups

## Question
What should I know about hiccups and stopping or preventing hiccups?

### Answer
Hiccups usually go away on their own. But there are things you can try to stop or prevent hiccups. These things may not work for everyone.

## Question
What should I know about hiccups and treatment from your gp?

### Answer
Your GP can check if a health condition or medicine causes your hiccups.

If they find a cause, they may recommend:

- treatment for the condition
- changing your medicine

Your GP may prescribe a medicine called chlorpromazine if:

- your hiccups last longer than 2 days
- there is no obvious cause

But not everyone can take chlorpromazine. In rare cases, they may prescribe baclofen.

## Question
What should I know about hiccups and why we get hiccups?

### Answer
Often there is no clear cause of hiccups.

Things that may trigger hiccups include:

- eating and drinking
- stress
- strong emotions, such as excitement

In rare cases, hiccups can last longer than a few minutes.

Hiccups that last longer than 2 days can be caused by a:

- health condition
- certain medicines
